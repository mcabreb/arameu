## Elevator Pitch

Arameu is an Android app that teaches Biblical Aramaic from zero through short, structured lessons — like Duolingo, but focused entirely on the language of Daniel and Ezra. Built as a personal gift for a single learner (Nuria), it strips away gamification and social features to focus purely on the learning: reading, writing, listening, and speaking, progressing from the alphabet to real Biblical passages.

## Business Objectives

- Deliver a working MVP (Units 1-3: alphabet, sounds, basic nouns) by April 11, 2026 as a birthday gift
- Provide a complete beginner-to-intermediate Biblical Aramaic course (~20 units) over subsequent months
- Require zero ongoing server costs — fully offline, bundled content
- Require zero financial spend beyond development time

## Proposed Approach

A native Android app (Kotlin + Jetpack Compose) with a linear course structure: units → lessons → exercises. Each lesson is 5-8 minutes. Content progresses through four levels (Foundations, Building Blocks, Sentences, Scripture) across ~20 units. English transliteration is the primary writing system; Square Aramaic script (identical to Hebrew block letters) is used for reading and writing exercises via the phone's Hebrew keyboard.

Audio is AI-generated using free-tier or open-source TTS. Speaking exercises use a self-assessed listen-and-repeat model. All content is bundled locally with SQLite for storage and progress tracking. No server, no accounts, no cloud sync.

## Key Constraints

- **Timeline:** MVP (Units 1-3) by April 11, 2026 — 8 days from project start
- **Budget:** Zero financial spend. Free tools, free TTS, sideloaded APK (no Play Store fee)

- **Content:** Biblical Aramaic learning resources are scarce — course content must be researched and authored as part of development
