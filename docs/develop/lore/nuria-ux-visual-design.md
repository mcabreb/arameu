# Nuria — UX & Visual Design Principles

The app's look and feel, as described by Nuria herself. Every design decision should honour these principles.

## The Feeling

The app should feel like **coming home to a beautiful book**. Welcoming, calm, clear. A space that is glad you're there. Not a classroom, not a checklist — a place that was waiting for you.

## Aesthetic References

- **Home** — personal, warm, familiar. You know where everything is.
- **Top-tier literature** — the craft is visible. Generous margins, beautiful typography, breathing room. You can feel the care on every page. You trust it immediately.
- **Ancient materials** — not digital, not modern. Handmade. From the ground.

## Colour Palette

- **Terracotta** — the dominant warm tone. The colour of Mediterranean walls, clay pots, ancient earth. Connects the language to the land it came from.
- **Parchment cream** — soft, warm background tones.
- **Aged ink / dark ochre** — for text. Soft against the warm background. Not harsh black. Like ink that has aged on a manuscript.
- This is a *material* palette, not a tech palette. It should feel like an ancient library manuscript.

## Layout

- **Minimal** — just the next thing waiting for you. No menus, no level pickers, no option overload.
- **One thing at a time** — like a good book, you continue where you left off. The app doesn't ask you to choose. It simply presents the next lesson.
- **Generous whitespace** — breathing room everywhere. The design respects attention through restraint.

## Interaction & Feedback

- **Quiet acknowledgement** — when the user gets something right, a gentle nod. No fanfare, no confetti, no dopamine traps.
- **Respectful progression** — move forward gracefully. Like a good teacher who doesn't make a fuss.
- **No gamification** — the language itself is the reward. Curiosity drives return, not points or streaks.

## Design Philosophy

Craft is in the care, not the decoration. Simplicity as respect. Ancient materials, welcoming space, one thing at a time.
