# Nuria — Learner Profile & App Soul

The app is built for Nuria. Every design decision should serve her experience.

## Who Nuria Is

- A genuine lover of languages, drawn to Aramaic by curiosity — not obligation
- Fascinated by the full experience: how Aramaic sounds, how it is written, its legacy in other living languages
- Starting from zero — no prior knowledge of Aramaic structure or Semitic languages
- Driven by wonder, not deadlines or exams

## How Nuria Learns

- **Ear-first**: she listens before she reads. Sounds settle in before rules arrive.
- **Pattern learner**: show her the root system (three-consonant roots) and she builds words herself. Logic underneath, not memorisation on top.
- **Watch, listen, repeat**: her natural method. Videos, audio, spoken repetition.
- **No gamification**: curiosity is the motivation. The language itself is the reward.

## The Core Insight

What brings Nuria back is **the feeling of saying something ancient out loud for the first time** — a connection across three thousand years. The magic is in the moment of speaking it.

## Lesson Design Principles

1. **Hear it** — every lesson begins with listening. The sound comes first.
2. **See the pattern** — reveal the root system, the logic, the structure underneath.
3. **Say it aloud** — every lesson ends with Nuria's voice, not a multiple choice answer.
4. **Build around voice, not screen** — the spoken word is the centre of the experience.
5. **Each lesson is an encounter** — not a test. Nuria should feel she is discovering something ancient and making it her own.
